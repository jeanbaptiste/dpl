How can we design technologies for profound human connection across distance?

A joint research program at the MIT Media Lab and the Human-IST Institute pioneering technologies to augment intelligence and foster rich human-to-human communication.

The Distributed Presence Lab (DPL) is a joint research initiative pioneering technologies—spanning sensors, actuators, and multimodal interfaces—to reinvent the interaction between our physical and digital worlds.

We see an urgent need to move beyond simple video conferencing and develop technologies that can transmit the full spectrum of human communication. Our goal is to create a future where distance is no longer a barrier to genuine human connection, creativity, and shared endeavors.

Check out DPL on the MIT Media Lab Page and the Human-IST Page.

Research Focus

The goals of the DPL program are ambitious and multifaceted. We aim to establish a new research field and community dedicated to the study of distributed presence and the augmentation of human connection by tackling topics such as:

Embodied Communication
Shared Environments
Tangible Interaction
Sensory Augmentation
Emotional Connection
Collaborative Creativity

Our Methods

In pursuit of our goals, we promote three core methods of inquiry to drive innovation and understanding.

Imagine

Design and deploy novel experiences that connect people in profound and unexpected ways.

Prototype

Develop novel technologies to capture, transmit, and render the subtleties of human presence.

Reflect

Share with partners how design choices affect people’s behavior, understanding, and sense of connection.
